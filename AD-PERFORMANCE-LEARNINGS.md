## Key Findings

### 1. Campaign Objective Matters
- **Best performers used `LINK_CLICKS`** objective (165k, 45k, 43k, 39k campaigns)
- Current campaigns use `OUTCOME_TRAFFIC` with `LANDING_PAGE_VIEWS` optimisation
- LINK_CLICKS is simpler — Facebook optimises for people who click, tends to be cheaper with more reach

### 2. Budget Concentration > Budget Spreading
- **The 165k viral post:** £20 over just 4 days = ~£5/day — aggressive spend helps Facebook's algorithm learn fast
- **Current approach:** £25 over 14 days = ~£1.79/day — too spread out, slow conservative delivery
- **Sweet spot:** Higher daily spend for shorter periods (7 days max)
- **New approach (Mar 2026):** £50 over 7 days = ~£7.14/day

### 3. The Viral Snowball Effect
- The 165k campaign had a CPC of **£0.004** — essentially free clicks
- This means the post went **organic/viral** after the initial paid push
- A strong post with good early engagement gets shared and amplified for free by Facebook
- Posts that don't get early engagement stay in paid-only territory

### 4. Interest Targeting Helps
- March 2026 campaigns had **no interest targeting** — just age 18+ and country list
- The older "Tailored web traffic" campaigns likely had interest-based targeting
- **Added interests (Mar 2026):** 3D printing, Architecture, Home decor, Interior design, Scale model, Miniature

### 5. Content Quality is King
- The full-colour 3D print post got 165k impressions for £20 — content drove virality
- The "demo capability" post spent £100 but only got 27k impressions with a terrible £0.20 CPC — weak content wastes budget
- Posts featuring impressive physical prints perform best
- Halloween/seasonal content (Ghost Bundles) also performed well

### 6. What Makes a Good Ad Post
- Show the finished physical product clearly
- Mention the building/landmark name (people search for these)
- Full-colour and multi-colour prints get more engagement
- Seasonal tie-ins work well (Halloween ghosts did great)
- Keep copy authentic and enthusiastic, not corporate

## Campaign Settings Reference

### Previous Best Settings (from top performers)
- **Objective:** LINK_CLICKS
- **Buying type:** AUCTION
- **Duration:** 4-7 days
- **Budget:** £20-30 per campaign
- **Result:** 40k-165k impressions

### Current Settings (Updated March 2026)
- **Objective:** OUTCOME_TRAFFIC (LINK_CLICKS deprecated in newer API)
- **Optimisation:** LANDING_PAGE_VIEWS
- **Billing:** IMPRESSIONS
- **Bid strategy:** LOWEST_COST_WITHOUT_CAP
- **Budget:** £50 lifetime over 7 days (~£7.14/day)
- **Age:** 18-65
- **Countries:** GB, US, CA, AU, DE, FR, IT, ES, NL, BE, AT, SE, NO, DK, FI, CH, IE, PL, PT, JP
- **Interest targeting:** 3D printing, Architecture, Home decor, Interior design, Scale model, Miniature
- **Advantage audience:** Enabled (lets Facebook expand beyond interests if it finds good results)

## Recommendations Going Forward

1. **One strong campaign per month** rather than two weak ones
2. **7-day duration max** to concentrate daily spend
3. **Pick the best listing** — quality content with clear, impressive photos
4. **Always include interest targeting** — don't rely on Advantage Audience alone
5. **Monitor early** — if a post gets cheap engagement in first 24h, it might go viral; if not, consider swapping creative
6. **Seasonal content** works well — plan posts around holidays/events
